10 Things I Check Before Copying a Trader on eToro

By Tom · Social Trading Vlog · Based on 9 years of copy trading

I've been copy trading on eToro since 2017. In that time I've copied dozens of traders — some brilliant, some disastrous. After losing money on flashy profiles with short track records, I developed this checklist. It's not a guarantee, but it's saved me from many bad decisions. Here are the 10 things I now check every single time before I hit the copy button.

1

At least 2 years of trading history

Anyone can have a good quarter. I want to see how they performed through at least one downturn. Two years gives you enough data to see if returns are consistent or just lucky. I don't copy anyone who's been on the platform for less than 2 years.

Where to check: Their profile chart — switch to "All time" view.
2

Risk score consistently below 5

eToro assigns every trader a risk score from 1-10. I aim for traders with a score that stays between 2-5. If someone's risk score spikes above 6 regularly, they're taking big bets — and big bets eventually go wrong. Consistency here is more important than the number itself.

Where to check: Stats tab → Risk Score chart. Look at the monthly breakdown.
3

No catastrophic drawdowns

Look at the chart for sudden sharp drops. A 30%+ drawdown in a single period is a red flag — even if they recovered. It means they were either over-leveraged or didn't manage risk properly. I prefer traders whose worst month is no worse than -10% to -15%.

Where to check: Their profile chart. Look for steep cliff-like drops.
4

Steady annual returns of 10-30%

This was the hardest lesson I learned. Traders showing 100%+ annual returns are almost always taking enormous risk. The traders who've made me consistent money aim for 10-30% annually. It sounds boring — but boring is profitable over years.

Where to check: Stats tab → Annual performance figures.
5

Active communication with copiers

Good traders post regular updates explaining their decisions, especially during tough periods. If someone goes silent when markets drop, that's concerning. I want to know the person behind the trades is engaged and transparent about their strategy.

Where to check: Their feed/posts. How often do they post? Do they explain losses?
6

Diversified portfolio (not all one sector)

If a trader has 80% of their portfolio in tech stocks or crypto, one bad sector rotation could wipe out their gains. I look for reasonable diversification across asset types or at least across sectors. Not perfect balance, just not a single massive bet.

Where to check: Portfolio tab → Asset breakdown pie chart.
7

Real copier numbers (and are they growing?)

A trader with 5,000+ copiers who's been growing steadily is a better sign than one with 200 copiers who had a viral moment. The crowd isn't always right, but sustained copier growth over years usually indicates consistent, trustworthy performance.

Where to check: Stats tab → Copiers chart over time.
8

They invest their own money meaningfully

Traders on eToro can show how much of their own capital is invested. If someone is trading with $500 of their own money while managing millions in copies, their incentives might not align with yours. I prefer traders who have meaningful skin in the game.

Where to check: About tab — some traders share their investment philosophy here.
9

Profitable months outnumber losing months

Nobody wins every month. But over 2+ years, I want to see significantly more green months than red ones — ideally 60-70%+ profitable months. This tells me the trader has a repeatable edge, not just a few big wins masking many losses.

Where to check: Stats tab → Monthly returns breakdown.
10

I understand their strategy (at least broadly)

If I can't understand what a trader does — even at a high level — I don't copy them. Long-term stock investing, dividend strategies, cautious forex — these I can understand. Complex derivatives or mysterious "AI-driven algorithms" without explanation? I'll pass.

Where to check: About tab → Investment strategy description. Their feed posts.
