Answering Your Questions — ETH Staking, Withdrawal Limits & Euro Accounts

Thought I'd answer a few viewer questions in this one :) Hope you all had a very Merry Christmas!

How to Set Up ETH Staking on eToro

Howard asked how I set up Ethereum staking. It's straightforward — once you own ETH on eToro (you've got to actually have an Ethereum trade open and be holding the asset as part of your portfolio), you go to Settings > Trading and you'll find the "ETH staking" program toggle.

If you decide to turn it on, your Ethereum starts earning staking rewards, (it's like a form of interest - ish) which I believe are around 2.5% depending on your club tier (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond - have a look at the club tier chart to see the details...)

But before you switch it on — check the risks

eToro lists several risks associated with staking ETH on the site, and the one that possibly matters most to some people is liquidity risk. During periods of high market volatility, you might not be able to unstake and sell your ETH as quickly as you'd like. Your "liquid" assets (meaning ones which are available to you now) might not be as liquid as you think...

So bear in mind that you may have to wait once you unstake your ETH before you can sell it and do whatever else you want to do with the money.

How long would you have to wait?

I'm not sure — it's very vague about how long any delay could be — could be that it's dependent on conditions which they can't accurately foresee. Worth bearing in mind in case your staked ETH forms part of your 'dry powder' which people sometimes set aside for interesting unexpected market opportunities.

There are also 'slashing' risks related to the Ethereum network itself — I'm not sure how much of a threat that one is. I believe slashing has to do with the uptime of the servers processing blocks for the ETH blockchain. It's not something I've really tried or looked into too much, so better check it out thoroughly first — it did worry me though so I wanted to mention it.

Why I turned staking off — tax

I actually had staking turned on and then switched it off. Why? Tax. Check with a professional in your jurisdiction how staking rewards are treated for tax purposes. It might be so little that the hassle of accounting for it isn't worth it. But tax rules are different in every jurisdiction and I guess it depends how much you could be making.

The Thomas Parry-Jones Situation

Thomas has done so well (51% for the year, 36,000 copiers, over $5 million in copy value) that eToro put a $20,000 minimum copy amount on him. I'm only copying him with an investment of $1,400 (now worth $2,155 thanks to his profits), which means I can't withdraw any partial amount. It's all or nothing.

If I was copying him with more than 20,000, then anything over that I'd be able to withdraw. For now, since I'm less than the 20,000 minimum, they've let me keep the copy open, but if I want to withdraw a single euro of it, I need to close the whole copy... Tough one, but glad I'm still able to copy him!

Why I had to stop copying Fund Manager Zek

That's actually why I had to stop copying Fund Manager Zek and others in the last update — I needed cash but didn't want to close my Thomas Parry-Jones copy. Thank you, Fund Manager Zek, for some amazing results.

Switching to the Euro Account

eToro now offers Euro accounts, which is brilliant for those of us in Europe. Previously, all accounts were in USD, meaning I'd pay forex conversion fees on every deposit and withdrawal. I've now transferred everything to my Euro account to avoid those fees going forward.

If you're in Europe, maybe it's worth looking into. Since the beginning of my time on eToro, I've always uploaded euros to my account and then they converted it into dollars — that actually may have worked out well over the years due to the strength of the dollar — not sure, as I didn't think about those things at first :) Good to see euros there though.
