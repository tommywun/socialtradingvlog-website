Ethereum's Comeback and the Great Crypto Debate

I started off the video with a little footage from around the North of Malta — check it out if you want to see a bit of the island. One of my subscribers suggested showing a bit of Malta before the videos, so it's there if you want to have a look!

ETH: from -$700 to +$815

Remember when my Ethereum was sitting at minus $700 in profit? Well it's now at plus $815, which is an unbelievable turnaround. ETH hit its all-time high recently and I'm still holding. At the time of filming, it's at $4,401 per Ethereum — a huge increase :) We'll see where it goes from here...

Everyone's trying to work out what's going on with crypto — there's a lot of bullish sentiment right now, but I'm not sure if anyone's quite sure why. Is it just that "Bitcoin cycle" again? The four-year cycle centred around the so-called "halving" event? It does seem to be, but maybe there's more...

Trump, stablecoins, and the bigger picture

President Trump has been changing laws rapidly over in the United States — pension funds can now buy into cryptos, there are new ETFs, and he signed the "GENIUS Act" paving the way for greater adoption. His family's "World Liberty Financial" project launched its USD1 stablecoin — another huge move into the space by them, and it's clear they've got huge plans. President Trump seems to want to bolster the US dollar by tying it to stablecoins, creating demand for dollars as each stablecoin is backed one-to-one by U.S dollars.

The conspiracy theorist in me wonders: is this a genuine embrace of decentralised finance, or a stepping stone toward central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) — programmable money that governments can control completely? Trump says he's against CBDCs, but there's definitely a power struggle happening. It's too hard for me to see what's really going on beneath the surface — at that level we're talking about the real movers and deciders in global economic policy. I can't see the plan at all, and I reckon if I try, I'll probably fall into some sort of mis-direction :)

Whatever happens, we're past the point where people can dismiss crypto as just a scam. When BlackRock is buying $100 million-worth at a time, there's clearly more to it.

He's down slightly since I copied him — am I worried? No. Long term, his history speaks for itself, so I'm happy to take the chance for now — as usual, we'll have to wait and see. Everything here is risky, and I have no idea how it'll play out, but for now, I'm taking the risk. We'll see!

My Overall Stats: 16.46% for the Year

I'm showing +16.46% for the year. Sounds decent, but is it really? If real inflation — including housing prices and everything they leave out of the official CPI — is higher than 6-7%, am I actually keeping up? I'm honestly not sure. Previous years were 0.67% and 5.58%, which almost certainly didn't beat inflation. At least this year looks better.

I think if the wider public really understood what inflation meant for their savings, there'd be more of an uproar about it — as things stand, people tie it to higher prices in shops which is bad enough, but not really the overall loss of purchasing power on the part of their savings. It's a real issue... It'd be so useful to have genuine reliable real-world inflation numbers so that at least we can see how much our investments need to be making in profit each year just so that we're not actually secretly losing money.

What I Look For in a Trader

A couple of thoughts about diversification

Over time, what I've come to value most isn't necessarily just sector diversification. In the beginning, when I heard about "diversification" I really thought that meant having exposure to different sectors and different parts of the overall economy — it makes sense — if one sector does badly, I'm not too exposed to it so that I'm risking too much of a loss.

But over the years, I've seen three traders all trading the same asset, one making a fortune and the other two losing. The problem wasn't so much what happened to the sector, but how the traders exposed to that sector reacted. It's about the person, not the market.

What I want to see is a trader who's uncorrelated with the broader market: profits when the market's up, and less loss (or even profit) when the market's down. That's the holy grail, and it's incredibly hard to find.
