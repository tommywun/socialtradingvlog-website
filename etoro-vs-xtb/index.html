eToro vs XTB — The Platform Quality Battle

By Tom  ·  Social Trading Vlog

XTB keeps appearing in trading forums as “the platform traders switch to.” After nearly 10 years on eToro, I investigated what makes XTB so popular — and whether it genuinely offers something eToro does not. The answer is nuanced: these are both strong all-rounders, but they excel in completely different areas.

Overview of Both Platforms

eToro was founded in 2007 and has over 35 million users globally. It is regulated by the FCA, CySEC, ASIC, and FinCEN. Its standout feature is CopyTrader — the ability to automatically mirror other investors' trades. It offers real stocks, ETFs, CFDs, and 80+ cryptocurrencies with real ownership.

XTB (X-Trade Brokers) was founded in 2002 in Poland and is publicly traded on the Warsaw Stock Exchange. It is regulated by the FCA (UK), KNF (Poland), CySEC, and several other authorities. XTB is known for its award-winning xStation 5 platform, competitive forex spreads, and commission-free stock trading up to €100,000 per month in turnover. It offers real stocks and ETFs, plus CFDs on forex, indices, commodities, and crypto.

Both are strong all-round platforms, but eToro leans towards social and copy trading, while XTB leans towards active trading with a superior platform experience.

Platform Showdown: xStation 5 vs eToro

This is the headline comparison. XTB's xStation 5 is widely regarded as one of the best proprietary trading platforms in the industry. It has won multiple awards for its charting capabilities, execution speed, and user experience. The platform includes advanced charting with drawing tools, market sentiment data built directly into the interface, a clean trade ticket, and real-time market analysis.

eToro's web platform is clean, social, and approachable. It is designed for accessibility rather than power. The social feed, CopyTrader interface, and portfolio view are intuitive. But eToro's charting tools are basic compared to xStation 5, and there is no built-in market sentiment data or advanced order types.

If I were purely evaluating the trading platform — ignoring copy trading entirely — xStation 5 is the better product. The charting is sharper, the execution feels faster, and the market sentiment data is genuinely useful. But I do not use eToro for its charting. I use it for copy trading, which xStation 5 does not have.

The fee structures are remarkably similar. Both offer commission-free stock trading, both charge the same currency conversion rate (0.5%), and both have a similar inactivity fee. The key differences: XTB has free withdrawals and no minimum deposit. XTB also pays interest on uninvested cash in some jurisdictions, which eToro does not.

XTB's commission-free stock trading has a €100,000/month turnover threshold — above that, a 0.2% commission applies. For most retail investors, this threshold is more than sufficient.

Stock Investing

Both platforms offer commission-free real stock and ETF investing. XTB provides access to 3,000+ stocks from 16 exchanges, while eToro offers 5,000+ instruments across ~17 exchanges. The selection is comparable for most investors.

XTB has a slight edge with fractional shares starting from just €1 (vs eToro's $10 minimum). XTB also offers a “Plans” feature for automated recurring investments, similar to a regular savings plan.

For basic stock investing, either platform works well. The differences are marginal. The decision between them really comes down to whether you want copy trading (eToro) or a better active trading platform (XTB).

Forex Trading: XTB's Clear Advantage

This is where XTB pulls significantly ahead. XTB offers 57+ forex pairs with spreads from 0.1 pips. The typical EUR/USD spread on XTB is around 0.9 pips, compared to eToro's typical 1.0 pip. On other pairs, the difference can be larger.

More importantly, xStation 5 is built for active forex trading. The execution is fast, the charting tools are professional-grade, and there is built-in market sentiment data showing what percentage of XTB traders are long or short on each pair. These are tools that active forex traders genuinely use.

eToro's forex offering is adequate but clearly secondary to its social trading features. The spreads are wider, the charting is basic, and there are no advanced order types like trailing stops with server-side execution.

If I were an active forex trader, I would use XTB over eToro without hesitation. The tighter spreads and better platform make a real difference when you are making frequent trades. But I am a copy trader, not a day trader — so the forex comparison does not change my personal choice.

Copy Trading: The Missing Feature

XTB does not offer copy trading. There is no way to automatically mirror another trader's positions on the platform. XTB has been developing community features, but as of now, there is nothing comparable to eToro's CopyTrader.

eToro's CopyTrader remains unique among major regulated brokers. You allocate a minimum of $200 to copy a trader, and every trade they make is automatically replicated in your portfolio proportionally. After nearly 10 years of using it, it continues to be the primary reason I stay on eToro.

If copy trading is your reason for investing, this comparison is decided before it starts. XTB is not an option.

Crypto: eToro's Territory

eToro offers 80+ cryptocurrencies with real ownership. You buy actual Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other coins. You can transfer them to the eToro Money crypto wallet. This is genuine crypto investing.

XTB offers crypto CFDs only in most regions. You are trading the price movement, not owning the underlying asset. You cannot withdraw or transfer crypto from XTB — it is purely a speculative trading instrument.

For anyone who wants to actually own and hold cryptocurrency, eToro wins decisively. XTB's crypto CFDs are suitable for short-term trading, but not for buy-and-hold crypto investors.

Education & Research

Both platforms have strong educational offerings, but with different approaches.

XTB Academy is comprehensive and well-structured. It includes video courses, articles, webinars, and a clear learning path from beginner to advanced. XTB's educational content is particularly strong on technical analysis and forex trading strategies. The built-in market analysis within xStation 5 — including daily market commentary and economic calendar — is excellent.

eToro has its own educational content, but its real strength is learning by doing — the social feed exposes you to other investors' reasoning, and copy trading lets you learn from experienced traders' actual decisions. eToro's academy covers the basics well but is less comprehensive than XTB's for active trading skills.

For structured education on trading techniques, XTB has the edge. For learning through social observation and copy trading, eToro's approach is unique.

Who Should Choose XTB?

Choose XTB if you:

  • Are an active forex or CFD trader who wants the tightest spreads
  • Value a professional-grade trading platform (xStation 5)
  • Want free withdrawals and no minimum deposit
  • Want interest on uninvested cash
  • Do not need copy trading or social features
  • Are focused on European stock investing under €100K/month
  • Want structured trading education (XTB Academy)

Choose eToro if you:

  • Want copy trading — automatically mirror experienced investors
  • Want to buy and hold real cryptocurrency
  • Value the social investing community and feed
  • Are a beginner who wants a simpler, more approachable interface
  • Want the Popular Investor programme (earn from being copied)
  • Want a single platform for stocks, crypto, and copy trading

Final Verdict

eToro and XTB are both excellent platforms that happen to excel in different areas. XTB is the better trading platform — superior charting, tighter forex spreads, a cleaner execution experience, and competitive costs. If you are an active, self-directed trader, XTB deserves serious consideration.

eToro is the better social investing platform — copy trading, real crypto, a community of investors, and an experience designed for accessibility. If copy trading matters to you, eToro remains the only serious option among regulated brokers.

There is no wrong choice here. The right platform depends on how you invest, not on which platform is objectively “better.”

Frequently Asked Questions

Is eToro or XTB better for forex trading?

XTB is significantly better for forex trading. Its xStation 5 platform offers spreads from 0.1 pips on EUR/USD (typical ~0.9 pips), compared to eToro's typical ~1.0 pip. XTB also has superior charting tools, faster execution, and more forex pairs. If forex is your primary focus, XTB is the stronger choice.

Does XTB have copy trading?

No. XTB does not currently offer copy trading. It is a self-directed trading platform. If copy trading — automatically mirroring another investor's trades — is important to you, eToro is the only major regulated platform that offers genuine CopyTrader functionality.

Can you buy real crypto on XTB?

In most regions, XTB only offers crypto CFDs — you do not own the underlying coins. eToro offers 80+ cryptocurrencies with real ownership, meaning you hold actual Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc. If owning real crypto matters to you, eToro is the better platform for that.

Which has lower fees — eToro or XTB?

Both offer commission-free stock trading. XTB has free withdrawals (eToro charges $5) and tighter forex spreads. Both charge the same currency conversion rate (0.5%) and similar inactivity fees. XTB also pays interest on uninvested cash in some jurisdictions. For forex traders, XTB is cheaper. For copy traders, eToro's fees are justified by the unique features.
