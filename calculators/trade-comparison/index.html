Frequently Asked Questions

Which platform has the lowest trading fees?
It depends on what you trade, how much, and where you live. For stocks, many platforms now offer zero-commission trading (eToro, Trading 212, Robinhood, Freetrade). However, these platforms may charge FX conversion fees on foreign stocks, or earn from the spread. For active traders, Interactive Brokers typically has the lowest all-in costs. Use the calculator above with your specific trade to find the cheapest option for you.
Are "commission-free" platforms really free?
No platform is truly "free" to use. Commission-free platforms earn money through: (1) the spread — the difference between buy and sell prices; (2) FX conversion fees when you buy assets in a foreign currency; (3) interest on your uninvested cash; (4) premium subscriptions; (5) payment for order flow (PFOF), where they sell your order data to market makers. The total cost of a trade is more than just the commission.
What is the difference between real stock ownership and CFDs?
When you buy real stocks, you own a share of the company — you may receive dividends and can hold indefinitely. With CFDs (Contracts for Difference), you're betting on the price movement without owning the asset. CFDs allow leverage (borrowing to trade) which amplifies both gains and losses. Most retail traders lose money trading CFDs. If you're a beginner, stick to real stock ownership.
Why do fees vary by country?
Different platforms are licensed to operate in different countries, and they may have different fee structures per region due to local regulations, currency conversion costs, and market access fees. For example, UK investors get ISA tax wrappers that aren't available elsewhere, and EU investors benefit from low-cost European exchange access through DEGIRO.
Should I choose the cheapest platform?
Not necessarily. Fees are important but they're not everything. Consider: (1) What assets do you want to trade? Not all platforms offer everything. (2) Do you want copy trading or social features? Only eToro and XTB offer this. (3) Is the platform regulated in your country? (4) How good is the app and customer support? (5) Does it offer tax-efficient accounts (ISA, SIPP)?
