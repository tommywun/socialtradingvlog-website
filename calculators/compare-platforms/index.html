Platform Details

Why I Use eToro

I've been using eToro since 2016 — nearly 10 years. It's the platform I know best, and its copy trading feature is genuinely unique. I copy 8-10 traders and it runs on autopilot. Is it perfect? No — the spreads on crypto are high, and the withdrawal fee used to be $5 (now free for some currencies). But for copy trading, there's nothing else that comes close.

That said, if you're purely buying stocks and don't care about copy trading, Trading 212 or DEGIRO might save you money on fees. And if you're an active trader who needs every asset class, Interactive Brokers is hard to beat. The best platform depends on what you need.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best social trading platform in 2026?
eToro is the most popular social trading platform with over 35 million users worldwide and the most fully-featured copy trading system. However, the "best" platform depends on your priorities. If you want copy trading, eToro is the clear leader. If you want the cheapest stock trading, consider Trading 212 or DEGIRO. For the widest range of assets and lowest overall fees for active traders, Interactive Brokers leads the pack.
Is eToro better than Plus500?
They serve different purposes. eToro offers real stock/ETF ownership, copy trading, and a social feed where you can follow other traders. Plus500 is CFD-only — you never own the underlying asset. Plus500 has no copy trading feature. If you want to own real shares or copy other traders, eToro is the better choice. If you only trade CFDs and want a simpler interface, Plus500 is worth considering.
Which platform has the lowest trading fees?
It depends on what you trade. For stocks, Trading 212 and eToro both offer zero-commission trading (though eToro earns from the spread). DEGIRO offers very low fees across European markets. For forex and CFDs, spreads vary significantly by asset — Interactive Brokers typically has the tightest spreads for active traders. Use our Fee Calculator to estimate eToro's specific costs for your trades.
Can I copy trade on platforms other than eToro?
eToro has the most comprehensive copy trading system, where you allocate a portion of your funds to automatically mirror another trader's positions. XTB has recently introduced a limited copy trading feature. Plus500, Trading 212, DEGIRO, and Interactive Brokers do not currently offer copy trading. Some third-party services offer copy trading via API connections to brokers, but these are more complex to set up.
Are these platforms regulated and safe?
All six platforms compared here are regulated by major financial authorities. eToro is regulated by the FCA (UK), CySEC (Cyprus), and ASIC (Australia). Plus500 is regulated by FCA, CySEC, and ASIC. Interactive Brokers is regulated by the SEC and FINRA (US), as well as FCA. Regulation doesn't eliminate risk, but it does mean your funds are protected up to certain limits under investor compensation schemes.
