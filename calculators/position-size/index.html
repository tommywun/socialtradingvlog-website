Why Position Sizing Matters

Diversification

Spreading your capital across multiple copy traders reduces the impact of any single trader having a bad run. If one trader loses 30%, it only affects a fraction of your portfolio when properly sized.

Risk Management

Setting maximum allocation limits per trader prevents overexposure. Conservative sizing with a cash reserve means you can weather drawdowns without being forced to stop copying during temporary dips.

Flexibility

Keeping cash in reserve lets you add new traders you discover, increase allocation to top performers, or take advantage of market dips. A fully deployed portfolio has no room to adapt.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I allocate per copy trader on eToro? ▼

A common approach is to allocate 5-20% of your portfolio per trader, depending on your risk tolerance and the number of traders you copy. Conservative investors typically keep each allocation under 10%, while moderate investors may go up to 20%. Always maintain a cash reserve of at least 5-20% for margin requirements and new opportunities.

What is the minimum amount to copy a trader on eToro? ▼

The minimum amount to copy a trader on eToro is $200. This means you need at least $200 per trader you want to copy. If your portfolio is $1,000 and you want to copy 5 traders, you would need the full amount just to meet the minimums with no cash reserve. Use this calculator to find the right balance.

How many traders should I copy on eToro? ▼

Most experienced copy traders recommend copying between 5-15 traders for good diversification. Copying fewer than 5 traders concentrates your risk, while copying more than 15 can dilute your returns and make it harder to monitor performance. The ideal number depends on your portfolio size and how actively you want to manage your allocations.

Should I split my copy trading portfolio equally or use different amounts? ▼

Both approaches have merit. Equal splitting is simpler and ensures balanced diversification. Risk-weighted allocation lets you put more money on traders you have higher conviction in, or less on higher-risk traders. Many copy traders start with equal splits and adjust over time based on each trader's performance and risk profile.
