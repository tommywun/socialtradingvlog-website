Frequently Asked Questions

What does 'copy open trades' mean on eToro?

When you enable 'copy open trades', your money is immediately used to buy into all the positions the trader currently holds, in proportion to your copy amount. If you don't enable it, you only copy new trades they open from that point forward.

Should I copy open trades when starting to copy someone on eToro?

It depends on the trader's style. For long-term investors who hold positions for months, copying open trades is usually fine. For short-term traders, you risk buying into positions that are already extended and may be near their target — so it's often safer to skip open trades.

What happens if I don't copy open trades on eToro?

You will only mirror trades the trader opens after you start copying them. You won't have exposure to their current portfolio, which means you may miss some gains but also avoid buying into overextended positions.
