Frequently Asked Questions

How do you take profits from copy trading on eToro?

You can take profits by either closing individual copied positions manually, stopping the copy entirely (which closes all positions), or withdrawing your available balance after closing trades. You can also remove funds from a copy to take partial profits without closing the entire copy.

When should I take profits on eToro?

There's no universal rule, but common approaches include closing when you've hit a target return (e.g. 20%), rebalancing periodically (e.g. annually), or closing when the trader's risk profile changes significantly. Tom recommends not timing the market but instead having a clear plan before you start copying.

Can I take partial profits from a copy trade on eToro?

Yes. You can remove funds from an active copy trade without closing it entirely. Go to your portfolio, open the copy, and select 'Edit'. From there you can reduce the copy amount and any freed-up funds will be returned to your available balance.
