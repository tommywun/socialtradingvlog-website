eToro vs DEGIRO — The Social Trader vs The Serious Investor

By Tom  ·  Social Trading Vlog

eToro and DEGIRO could not be more different. One is a social trading platform built around copy trading and community. The other is a serious, no-frills brokerage built for self-directed investors who want access to global markets at the lowest possible cost. After nearly 10 years on eToro, I explored DEGIRO to understand who it serves — and whether it is genuinely better for certain investors.

Overview of Both Platforms

eToro was founded in 2007 and has grown to over 35 million users globally. It is regulated by the FCA (UK), CySEC (Cyprus), ASIC (Australia), and FinCEN (US). eToro is best known for its CopyTrader feature — the ability to automatically mirror the trades of other investors. It offers real stocks, ETFs, CFDs across multiple asset classes, and 80+ cryptocurrencies with real ownership.

DEGIRO was founded in the Netherlands in 2013 and merged with flatexDEGIRO AG in 2020, making it part of a publicly traded German financial group listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange. It is regulated by BaFin (Germany) and the AFM (Netherlands) under MiFID II. DEGIRO provides direct market access to 50+ stock exchanges worldwide and offers stocks, ETFs, bonds, options, futures, and warrants. It does not offer CFDs, forex, or crypto.

These platforms serve fundamentally different types of investor. Understanding that difference is the key to choosing between them.

The Fundamental Difference

eToro is a social trading platform. Its core value proposition is community and copy trading. You browse other investors, analyse their performance, and copy the ones you trust. The social feed, Popular Investor programme, and Smart Portfolios all revolve around this social ecosystem. You can also trade independently, but the platform is designed around the social experience.

DEGIRO is a traditional discount brokerage. There is no social feed, no copy trading, no community features. You research your own investments, place your own trades, and manage your own portfolio. DEGIRO's value proposition is low-cost access to the widest possible range of global markets.

I think of it this way — eToro is where I go to copy experienced traders and benefit from their decisions. If I wanted to build a global stock portfolio myself, picking individual shares across different exchanges, DEGIRO would be the tool for that job. They are not competing for the same user.

On pure cost, DEGIRO wins in most categories. No withdrawal fee, no inactivity fee, and significantly lower currency conversion (0.25% vs 0.5%). For a non-USD investor putting £10,000 into US stocks, the conversion cost difference alone is £25 — DEGIRO costs £25 in conversion, eToro costs £50.

Where eToro's fees make sense is when you factor in the features. Copy trading, crypto, and CFD access are included in eToro's cost structure. DEGIRO simply does not offer these. You are comparing the cost of two different products.

If all you want is to buy and hold stocks, DEGIRO is objectively cheaper. But if you want copy trading, you would need to pay for both DEGIRO and a separate copy trading service — which does not really exist at DEGIRO's price point. eToro bundles it all together.

Available Markets & Exchanges

This is DEGIRO's knockout advantage. DEGIRO provides direct market access to 50+ stock exchanges across Europe, North America, Asia-Pacific, and beyond. You can buy shares directly on the Tokyo Stock Exchange, the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, the Australian Securities Exchange, multiple European exchanges (XETRA, Euronext Amsterdam, London Stock Exchange, Borsa Italiana), and many more.

eToro offers around 5,000+ instruments across approximately 17 exchanges. For most casual investors, this is plenty. But if you want to invest in a specific Japanese small-cap stock, or a Scandinavian company listed only on the Stockholm exchange, DEGIRO is the one that gives you access.

Options & Futures: DEGIRO's Exclusive

DEGIRO offers real options trading at €0.75 per contract and futures trading at the same rate. These are genuine listed derivatives on European exchanges — not the synthetic CFD products that some platforms offer.

eToro does not offer options or futures trading at all. If derivatives are part of your investment strategy, DEGIRO (or Interactive Brokers) is where you need to be.

I do not personally trade options or futures, so this is not something that draws me to DEGIRO. But I know from my YouTube audience that this is a significant factor for more experienced investors. If you need these instruments, the choice is straightforward.

Copy Trading: eToro's Exclusive

DEGIRO has absolutely no copy trading, social trading, or any community features. There is no way to see what other investors are doing, no way to automatically mirror anyone's trades, and no social feed.

eToro's CopyTrader is a genuinely unique feature in the regulated brokerage space. You allocate a minimum of $200 to copy a trader, and every trade they make is automatically replicated in your account proportionally. After nearly 10 years of using it, I can say it works as advertised — though choosing the right traders is where the real skill lies.

If copy trading is your primary reason for investing, this comparison is already decided. DEGIRO is not even in the conversation.

Crypto: eToro Only

DEGIRO does not offer cryptocurrency trading of any kind. No real crypto, no crypto CFDs, nothing. If you want crypto exposure through DEGIRO, your only option is buying a crypto ETF or ETP — an indirect investment vehicle that tracks crypto prices.

eToro offers 80+ cryptocurrencies with real ownership. You can buy, hold, and transfer Bitcoin, Ethereum, and dozens of altcoins. This is genuine crypto ownership, not a CFD or derivative.

For investors who want a single platform covering stocks, ETFs, and crypto, eToro is the obvious choice. DEGIRO does not pretend to be a crypto platform.

Platform & User Experience

eToro's interface is designed for accessibility. The social feed, copy trading features, and clean design make it approachable for people who have never invested before. The mobile app is polished and the web platform is intuitive.

DEGIRO's interface is functional but more utilitarian. It is not ugly, but it is clearly designed for people who know what they are looking for. There is less hand-holding, fewer educational prompts, and a more traditional brokerage feel. The mobile app works well but lacks the visual polish of eToro's.

For beginners, eToro is the friendlier experience. For experienced investors who just want to place a trade quickly without social features, DEGIRO gets out of your way more effectively.

Which One Is Right for You?

Choose eToro if you:

  • Want copy trading — the ability to automatically mirror other investors
  • Are a beginner who wants a friendly, guided experience
  • Want to trade crypto alongside stocks
  • Want CFD access for forex, commodities, or leveraged positions
  • Value the social community and investor feed
  • Want everything on one platform with a simple interface

Choose DEGIRO if you:

  • Are a self-directed investor who does their own research
  • Want access to 50+ global stock exchanges
  • Need options, futures, or bond trading
  • Want the lowest possible fees, especially currency conversion
  • Do not need or want social features or copy trading
  • Are a European investor focused on building a diversified stock portfolio
  • Value the backing of a German-regulated banking group

Final Verdict

This is not a case of one platform being better than the other. eToro and DEGIRO serve genuinely different investors with minimal overlap.

If copy trading, crypto, and a social investing experience are important to you, eToro is the clear choice. DEGIRO does not even attempt to compete in this space.

If you are a self-directed investor who wants the widest possible access to global markets at the lowest cost, with options and futures available, DEGIRO is superior. eToro cannot match DEGIRO's exchange coverage or fee structure for pure stock investing.

Personally, I use eToro because copy trading is central to my investing approach — it is what I have been documenting for nearly 10 years on YouTube. But I respect DEGIRO as a platform and understand exactly why serious buy-and-hold investors prefer it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is eToro or DEGIRO better for beginners?

eToro is more beginner-friendly. Its interface is simpler, it offers copy trading so you can mirror experienced investors, and it has more educational content. DEGIRO is designed for self-directed investors who already know what they want to buy. If you are completely new to investing, eToro is the easier starting point.

Does DEGIRO have copy trading?

No. DEGIRO does not offer any copy trading or social trading features. It is a pure self-directed brokerage focused on low-cost access to global stock exchanges. If copy trading is important to you, eToro is the only major regulated platform offering genuine CopyTrader functionality.

Can you trade crypto on DEGIRO?

No. DEGIRO does not offer cryptocurrency trading of any kind — neither real crypto nor crypto CFDs. If you want to buy and hold real cryptocurrency, eToro offers 80+ coins with actual ownership. DEGIRO focuses exclusively on traditional securities: stocks, ETFs, bonds, options, and futures.

Which has lower fees — eToro or DEGIRO?

DEGIRO is cheaper on most fee metrics. It has no withdrawal fee (eToro charges $5), no inactivity fee (eToro charges $10/month after 12 months), and lower currency conversion at 0.25% vs eToro's 0.5%. Both offer commission-free stock trading on major exchanges. DEGIRO wins on pure cost; eToro wins on features like copy trading and crypto.
